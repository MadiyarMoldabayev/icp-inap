## Mission & Purpose

Promoting a just and democratic society in Kazakhstan through objective sociological research, development of public policy recommendations, and advancement of international human rights standards. Working at the intersection of science and practice to shape evidence-based policy.

### Core Focus Areas

1. **Sociological Research** — Large-scale studies on national values, public opinion, quality of life
2. **Legal Reform** — Legislative reforms on women's rights, domestic violence, international standards
3. **International Cooperation** — UN participation, CEDAW advocacy, ILO Convention No. 190
4. **Gender Equality** — Fighting domestic violence, protecting women's and children's rights
5. **Evidence-Based Policy** — Using data and scientific analysis to shape public policy
6. **Rule of Law** — Strengthening judicial systems and access to justice
7. **Civil Society Development** — State-civil society relations, NGO capacity building

## Domestic Violence Statistics & Findings

- **104,076** victims of domestic violence registered in 2024
- **56,854** of them — women
- **3.5x–4x** increase in registered cases after 2024 Law
- **70** crisis centers operational across Kazakhstan
- **5,000+** women assisted by crisis centers in 2025
- **7-year gap** before domestic violence was criminalized in 2024

### Key Legislative Changes (2024):

- Criminalization of battery and intentional infliction of minor bodily harm
- Criminalization of harassment of persons under 16 (online and offline)
- Life imprisonment for rape of minor resulting in death
- Punishment for stalking and forced marriages

### Recommended Actions (4 Areas):

1. Further targeted legislative improvements
2. Inclusive rehabilitation/resocialization projects ("Housing first, then anything else")
3. Increased involvement of women in police/law enforcement (target: 30%)
4. Consistent outreach through NGOs

### Rehabilitation Components:

- Housing first
- Psychological support
- Resocialization planning
- Training and financial assistance
- Subsidized/accessible employment
- Child assistance in crisis
- Digital solutions
- Concept: "Investing in empathy"

## Gender Issues & Cultural Context

- **Women in parliament:** 18% (target: 30%)
- **Female ministers:** 2
- **Gender wage gap:** Women earn ~33% less than men
- **Life expectancy gap:** Women live 8 years longer than men
- **Pension disparity:** Women receive minimum pensions due to family care time
- **Cultural concept:** "Ұят" (moral shame) — constrains women's behavior more than men's
- **Justice system bias:** Male-dominated police force, judicial preference for property/divorce framing, women viewed as "emotional"
- **Traditional practices reframed:** Bride kidnapping, polygamy, bride price portrayed as "essential traditions"

## Civil Society in Kazakhstan

### NGO Statistics (2023):

- **23,335** total registered NGOs
- **18,204** active
- **2,217** inactive
- **1,103** ceased operations
- **7x growth** since 2003 (from 3,500)

### Civil Sector Development Phases:

1. **1991–1995:** "Anarchic Democracy" — high activity amid weak state power
2. **1995–2005:** Significant role changes, legalization, possible use for regime change
3. **2005–2022:** GONGO era (government-organized NGOs)
4. **2022–present:** Post-January events transformation — new Kurultai, constitutional reforms, ombudsman

### Key Issues:

- Discriminatory NGO legislation (registration, tax reporting)
- Government funding dependence
- Limited international donor access
- Government officials' lack of understanding of NGO sector
- State contract procedures not refined — mainly central cities
- Need for digitalization of development indicators
